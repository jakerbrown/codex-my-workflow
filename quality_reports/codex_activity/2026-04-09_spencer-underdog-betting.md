# Codex Activity: Spencer underdog betting

- Worked on a full empirical package testing whether early-season underdog
  betting historically made money in the NFL, NBA, NHL, and MLB.
- Built a reproducible pipeline from public sportsbook archive snapshots,
  cleaned the data, generated the main figures and tables, and drafted the
  methods memo, results memo, blog post, and paired Beamer/Quarto brief.
- This mattered because the idea is plausible on market-learning grounds, but
  it needed a benchmarked and uncertainty-aware test rather than a simple ROI
  anecdote.
- Verified the Python pipeline, the R robustness script, the Beamer compile,
  the Quarto render, and the replication rebuild. Remaining limitation: the
  cleanest common public source ends in 2021 and full-season comparisons rely
  on an archive-derived regular-season proxy.
