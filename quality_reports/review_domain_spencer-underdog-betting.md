# Domain Review: Spencer underdog betting

## Core judgment

The substantive conclusion is mostly well calibrated: the package does **not**
overstate the evidence for a profitable betting edge.

## Strengths

- Uses real historical moneyline data rather than simulation-first storytelling.
- Distinguishes:
  - absolute profitability
  - relative outperformance versus worse benchmarks
  - probability miscalibration versus payout asymmetry
- Avoids the lazy "positive sub-variant equals edge" move.

## Risks

- The regular-season proxy is archive-derived rather than official.
  - That matters mainly for full-season and later-season comparisons, not the
    headline early windows.
- The common public source stops in 2021.
  - That is a real external-validity limitation and should stay prominent.
- The `big_dog_3w` variant is interesting but easy to overread.
  - The draft currently treats it as exploratory, which is correct.

## What should be fixed before claiming quality 90

- Keep the final summary disciplined on one point:
  - Spencer's theory receives weak support as a mechanism story, not strong
    support as a betting recommendation.
- Make sure the final close explicitly says that the evidence is insufficient
  for belief in a durable tradable edge.

## Verdict

- No blocking domain problem found.
- Quality from a domain-review perspective: `90` if the final user-facing
  summary keeps the same cautious language as the current draft.
